About Me

I’m Lloyd Musa, a Computer Science student at York University’s Lassonde School of Engineering with a strong focus on software development, embedded systems, and applied AI. Growing up in Nigeria instilled in me resilience, adaptability, and creativity—traits that now fuel my technical and leadership work. At Lassonde, I’ve led student initiatives as a First-Year Representative at Bethune College Council, engineered impactful AI projects, and mentored peers through collaborative, hands-on learning. My approach blends deep technical execution with clear communication, always aiming to build systems that solve real problems and scale effectively.

Inspired by my older brother’s discipline and integrity, I strive to lead with curiosity, consistency, and a strong sense of purpose. I believe great software is born from empathy and refined through disciplined iteration. Whether I’m building tools for accessibility, crafting embedded firmware, or leading cross-functional teams, I ground my work in reflection and growth. As Malcolm X said, “The future belongs to those who prepare for it today”—a principle I live by in everything I create.
